Mentorship Program

Learning the platform is half the journey. Knowing how the industry works, building a network and believing you belong — that's the other half. Our mentors have walked this road, and they walk it again with you.

Find a Mentor    Become a Mentor

How Mentorship Works Here

We match each participant with an experienced professional from the Salesforce ecosystem — admins, consultants, analysts and architects who volunteer their time. Matches are based on your goals, language, background and the mentor's expertise. You meet regularly one-on-one, set concrete goals together and review progress over a structured mentorship cycle.

Many of our mentors come from the same backgrounds as our participants: they migrated to a new country, changed careers late, or fought to be taken seriously in tech. That shared experience is what makes the guidance real.

What Your Mentor Helps With

Mentorship is tailored to you — these are the most common focus areas.

Learning Path Guidance

Which certification next, which skills matter for your target role, and how to avoid wasting months on the wrong track.

CV & LinkedIn Review

Presenting your experience — including non-tech experience — in the language recruiters in the ecosystem actually search for.

Interview Preparation

Mock interviews, common Salesforce interview questions and honest feedback before the real thing.

Network Building

Introductions to community groups, events and people — most first jobs in the ecosystem come through connections.

Confidence & Belonging

A safe space to ask "beginner" questions and a regular reminder that you are not an impostor.

Work-Life Balance

Sustainable study routines and realistic expectations — burning out before your first job helps no one.

The Mentorship Cycle

1

Apply & Match

Tell us your goals; we pair you with the most suitable volunteer mentor.

2

Kick-off

First meeting: expectations, goals and a meeting rhythm you both commit to.

3

Regular Sessions

One-on-one sessions over the cycle, with concrete tasks between meetings.

4

Review & Next Step

Progress review and a plan: internship, job applications or another cycle.
