Tips for Preparing for the Salesforce Admin Certification

What actually works when preparing for your first Salesforce exam — from people who've coached hundreds through it.

The Salesforce Certified Administrator exam is the most common first certification in the ecosystem — and for newcomers, usually the most stressful. Having coached many cohorts through it, here is the advice we repeat most often.

1. Start from the official exam guide

Salesforce publishes an exam guide listing every topic area and its weight in the exam. Print it, and treat it as your syllabus. Most failed first attempts trace back to studying what was interesting instead of what is weighted.

2. Practice beats reading — every time

For every hour you read or watch videos, spend an hour doing the thing in your free Developer org. Create the profile. Build the flow. Break the validation rule and fix it. The exam asks scenario questions, and scenarios are exactly what hands-on practice trains you for.

3. Use mock exams as diagnosis, not reassurance

Take a practice exam early — even before you feel ready. The goal isn't the score; it's the map of your weak areas. Review every wrong answer until you can explain why the right one is right. Then study, and repeat.

4. Learn to read the questions

Certification questions are famously wordy. Train yourself to find the actual requirement in the scenario, eliminate the two obviously wrong options first, and watch for qualifiers like "most efficient" or "without code" — they usually decide the answer.

5. Make a realistic schedule — then protect it

Consistency beats intensity. Forty-five focused minutes a day outperforms a six-hour Sunday marathon you'll dread and eventually skip. If you're studying alongside a job or family, build the plan around your real life — this is a core principle in all our training programs.

6. Don't study alone

A study group keeps you accountable, answers your questions faster than a search engine, and reminds you that everyone finds sharing rules confusing at first. Our cohorts and community exist for exactly this.

7. Book the exam date before you feel ready

Nobody ever feels ready. A date on the calendar converts intention into preparation. If needed, exams can be rescheduled — but an unbooked exam is postponed forever.
