Salesforce Business Analyst Program

Business analysts are the bridge between what an organization needs and what the platform can do. If you enjoy understanding problems, talking to people and designing solutions, this role may fit you better than any purely technical path.

Apply Now

What Does a Salesforce Business Analyst Do?

A Salesforce Business Analyst works with stakeholders to understand how a business operates, identifies where the platform can improve those operations, and documents requirements so that admins and developers can build the right solution. They map processes, write user stories, run workshops, test what gets built and make sure it actually solves the original problem.

It is one of the fastest-growing roles in the ecosystem, and it particularly rewards skills many career changers already have: communication, domain knowledge from previous industries, organization and empathy. Salesforce offers an official Business Analyst certification, and our program is aligned with it.

What You Will Learn

Practical business analysis skills applied directly to Salesforce projects.

Requirements Gathering

Interview techniques, workshops and discovery sessions that surface what stakeholders really need — not just what they say.

Process Mapping

Documenting current and future business processes with clear diagrams that both business and technical teams understand.

User Stories & Backlog

Writing clear, testable user stories with acceptance criteria, and managing a prioritized backlog.

Salesforce Platform Knowledge

Enough hands-on admin knowledge to know what the platform can and cannot do — essential for realistic requirements.

Testing & UAT

Planning and running user acceptance testing, writing test cases and managing feedback loops before go-live.

Agile Ways of Working

How real Salesforce project teams work: sprints, ceremonies, collaboration tools and the BA's role in each.
