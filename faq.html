Frequently Asked Questions

Everything organizations ask us most — about Salesforce, CRM projects and working with us.

About Salesforce & CRM

What is Salesforce?

Salesforce is the world's leading cloud-based customer relationship management (CRM) platform. Organizations use it to manage sales, customer service, marketing, e-commerce and analytics on one platform, accessed through a browser or mobile app with nothing to install.

How does a CRM help our sales and growth?

A CRM puts every customer interaction — calls, emails, offers, orders — on one record. Salespeople stop losing leads in inboxes, managers see the real pipeline instead of estimates, and forecasting becomes data instead of gut feeling. The organizations we work with typically see faster follow-ups, fewer dropped opportunities and much clearer reporting within months.

Why Salesforce and not another CRM?

Three reasons come up in every evaluation: the platform's flexibility (it can be configured to almost any process without code), the ecosystem around it (thousands of ready-made apps on AppExchange, a huge talent pool) and its investment in innovation, including AI. That said, our consulting is honest — if your needs are simple, we'll say so.

Is Salesforce suitable for small organizations?

Yes. Editions and pricing scale from a few users to thousands, and you only pay for what you use. For non-profits the entry point is even lower thanks to donated licenses. The key is starting with a right-sized implementation instead of building everything on day one.

What are the advantages of a cloud platform?

No servers to buy or maintain, automatic upgrades three times a year, access from anywhere on any device, and enterprise-grade infrastructure that a small organization could never afford to run itself.

Is our data secure on Salesforce?

Salesforce provides enterprise-grade security: encryption, granular access controls, audit trails and compliance certifications. In practice, most risk sits in configuration — which is why we design your permission model carefully and document it.

Can we use Salesforce on mobile?

Yes — the Salesforce mobile app gives your team access to records, dashboards and approvals from their phone, and we configure it as part of every implementation where field work matters.

Projects & Technical

Can Salesforce integrate with our ERP?

Yes. ERP integration is one of our core services — customers, orders and invoices synced automatically so both systems agree. See our Systems Integration page for how we approach it.

Does it work with Outlook and Gmail?

Yes — native integrations log emails and meetings onto the right Salesforce records automatically, so the CRM stays complete without extra typing.

Can our own business processes be reflected in the platform?

That's the point of Salesforce: custom objects, fields, page layouts and no-code automation (Flow) let us model your actual process. Where clicks genuinely aren't enough, custom development fills the gap — sparingly and documented.

What is AppExchange?

Salesforce's app marketplace: thousands of ready-made add-ons for e-signature, accounting, marketing and more. Often the fastest answer to a requirement is an app that already exists — we help you choose safely.

Can you migrate our existing data?

Yes — data migration from spreadsheets and legacy systems is part of nearly every project. We clean, deduplicate and validate during the move, because migrating dirty data just relocates the problem.

How long does an implementation take?

It depends on scope: a focused NPSP or Sales Cloud setup can go live in weeks, while multi-system programs take months. After the discovery call, you get a written plan with a realistic timeline — and we deliver in short iterations so you see progress from the first weeks.

Working With Us

How does pricing work?

Two separate costs: Salesforce licenses (paid to Salesforce — with significant donations and discounts available for eligible non-profits) and our services. As a non-profit consultancy we price to cover our costs and fund our mission, not to maximize margin. Every engagement starts with a written scope and budget.

What makes a non-profit consultancy different?

Our delivery teams pair experienced consultants with talented newcomers we've trained — people from diverse and minority backgrounds entering the tech industry. You get quality work at fair rates; your project creates someone's first career opportunity. Impact is built into the invoice.

What happens after go-live?

We don't disappear. Every project ends with training, documentation and a handover — and if you want ongoing help, our managed support covers user management, small changes and continuous improvement.

We already have Salesforce but it's underused. Can you help?

Very common, and yes. We run health checks on existing orgs: what's configured, what's actually used, where users struggle — followed by a prioritized fix list. Often the platform isn't the problem; the setup is.

How do we start?

With a free discovery call. Tell us about your organization and goals via the contact form, and we'll take it from there — no obligation, no hard sell.
