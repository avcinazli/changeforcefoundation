Frequently Asked Questions

Everything people ask us most — about Salesforce, careers in the ecosystem and our programs.

About Salesforce & Careers

What is Salesforce?

Salesforce is the world's leading cloud-based customer relationship management (CRM) platform. Organizations use it to manage sales, customer service, marketing, e-commerce and analytics in one place. Because it runs entirely in the cloud, there is nothing to install — users access it through a browser or mobile app.

Why build a career in the Salesforce ecosystem?

The ecosystem around Salesforce — partners, consultancies and customer companies — continuously needs administrators, analysts, consultants and developers. Certifications are recognized internationally, much of the work can be done remotely, and entry doesn't require a computer science degree. For people restarting or switching careers, few tech paths are as accessible.

Is Salesforce hard to learn?

The admin side of Salesforce is configured with clicks, not code, which makes it one of the more approachable technologies for beginners. Salesforce also provides Trailhead, a free gamified learning platform, which our curriculum builds on. Like any profession it takes consistent effort — most of our participants reach certification readiness in a matter of months of structured study.

Do I need programming knowledge?

No. Salesforce Administrator and Business Analyst roles are declarative — you configure the platform through its interface. Programming (Apex, JavaScript) only becomes relevant if you later choose the developer path.

What roles exist in the ecosystem?

Common roles include Administrator, Business Analyst, Consultant, Developer, Marketing Cloud Specialist, Solution Architect and Project Manager. Most people enter through the admin or analyst route and specialize from there.

What is a Salesforce certification and why does it matter?

Certifications are official exams by Salesforce that validate your platform knowledge — the Salesforce Certified Administrator is the most common first one. Employers use them as a baseline signal, especially for candidates without prior job experience in the ecosystem, which is exactly why our training is certification-oriented.

Can Salesforce work be done remotely?

Yes — a large share of ecosystem roles are remote or hybrid, since the platform itself is cloud-based. This flexibility is one reason we emphasize work-life balance in our programs.

Our Programs

Who can apply to your programs?

Our programs prioritize people from diverse and minority backgrounds — including migrants, young people, women and career changers — who face barriers entering tech. If that describes you, apply; if you're unsure, contact us and we'll talk it through.

Do I need any previous tech experience?

No. Our Salesforce Admin track is designed for complete beginners. Motivation and the ability to commit regular study time matter far more than your starting point.

How much do the programs cost?

As a non-profit, our goal is to remove financial barriers, not create them. Conditions can vary by program and cohort, so please contact us for the current details of the program you're interested in.

Are the programs online or in person?

Our training and mentorship are delivered primarily online, which lets us reach participants regardless of where they live. Contact us for the format of the current cohort.

How long does the training take?

Duration varies by track and cohort, and our schedules are designed to be combined with a job or family responsibilities. We'll share the current cohort's timeline during the application process.

Do you guarantee a job at the end?

No honest organization can guarantee employment. What we provide is what the market demands: certification-oriented training, real project experience through internships, mentorship and job search support. Our graduates' stories show the model works — but your effort is part of the equation.

How does the internship work?

After completing training, participants join supervised consulting projects we deliver for non-profit organizations — implementing NPSP, configuring CRM processes, migrating data. You work in a team under experienced supervision and finish with portfolio material and references. See the Internship Program page.

How are mentors matched?

We match based on your goals, language, background and the mentor's expertise. Many mentors share our participants' life experience — career changers mentoring career changers, migrants mentoring migrants.

What happens after I apply?

We review your application and invite you to a short conversation — not a technical test, but a chat about your goals, availability and fit. You'll then hear from us about the next cohort.

For Organizations & Supporters

We're an NGO — how can you help us with Salesforce?

We provide NonProfit Success Pack (NPSP), CRM and digitization consulting designed for non-profit budgets. Salesforce also offers donated and discounted licenses for eligible non-profits, and we can guide you through that process. Start with a free discovery call via our NonProfit Consulting page.

What is the NonProfit Success Pack (NPSP)?

NPSP is a free, open-source package built on Salesforce specifically for non-profits. It adds ready-made structures for donors, donations, recurring gifts, grants, households and volunteers — so NGOs don't have to build fundraising CRM from scratch.

How can I volunteer or become a mentor?

If you work in the Salesforce ecosystem and can offer a few hours a month, we'd love to have you as a mentor, trainer or project supervisor. Reach out through the contact form.

How can companies partner with you?

Companies support us by hiring our graduates, offering internship placements, sponsoring training cohorts or lending expert volunteers. If your company believes in a more diverse ecosystem, let's talk.

Is my data safe when I apply?

We process personal data only for program administration and in line with applicable data protection law. See our Privacy Notice for details.
